Applying to university in a foreign country can be daunting... However, thousands of international students around the world make it every year. You too can make it!

This site contains very basic information about the American university application process, for undergraduate studies (baccalaurat ou licence) and graduate studies (master ou doctorat).


About the American higher education system

In the U.S, bachelor's programs last four years, Master's degrees one to two years, and Ph.D. four to six years. Unlike many other countries, you cannot start medical school straight out of high school from your first year of university. First, you have to earn a four-year bachelor's degree in any major(facult), then apply to medical school(which lasts four years). The same process applies to dental, veterinary, or law school.

In the U.S, you are not required to declare your major until the second year of university, which means that by the time you start university, is okay to be undecided about what you want to study. Moreover, you can have more than one major or minor at no extra cost.


Differences and similarities between University, College and Community College

  • University: offers undergraduate degrees (bachelor's) and graduate degrees (master's degrees and/or PhD)
  • College: offers only undergraduate degrees. Therefore, you can get a bachelor's degree at a college or a university. Moreover, the terms "College" and "University" are interchangeably used in the United States
  • Community College: offers associate degrees in 2 years. You can transfer from a community college to a college or university if you want more advanced degrees.

In the US, the words "college" and "university" are often used interchangeably, and their cost of attendance is more or less similar. You can get your bachelor's degree at a university or a college.


Financial aid

American schools are notoriously expensive. Unless you are rich or willing to take loans, you will need financial aid. You should make sure the school that you are applying to normally gives financial aid. Admission is the most important step because you will need to be admitted before your school considers giving you a scholarship. Most schools will often fund your education if they like you as a student. There are different types of scholarships, but many international students often receive need-based scholarships.

So, where should you start this whole application process?

  • Look for universities that have majors that you are interested in
  • Look at the university's website the application requirements, especially standardized tests and language requirements for international students
  • If you know that you will need a scholarship, make sure the university provides scholarships
  • Study and prepare yourself for the standardized tests and get the best scores you can get
  • Make sure you have all the documents needed for your application
  • Submit your application and hope for the best!
To increase your chances, you might need to apply to many universities. Do not hesitate to contact the schools that you are interested in if you have any questions. It might also be helpful to talk to current students or alumni of the school that you are applying to.

As you prepare your undergraduate application, you should know that:
  • You need to do well in your classes so that your grades can help you to stand out among thousands of applicants
  • Your grades are not everything. You also need extracurricular activities to show that you are more than a good student
  • If English is not your native language, most schools will require you take an English test
  • Work hard to get above the minimum standardized test scores required by your schools
Here are some of the documents that you might need for your application:
  • High school diploma
  • National exam scores
  • High school transcripts (usually the last three years)
  • Recommendation letters

The most common standardized English test is called TOEFL. It has four sections: reading, writing, listening and speaking.
For more information about TOEFL, click here. Depending on the university, other alternative tests might be IELTS or Duolingo

Some schools will also require tests called SAT or ACT.

If you are not able to do any of those tests, there are a few schools (mostly community colleges), which do not require any standardized tests.


Admission and financial aid

Before you get a scholarship, you will need to apply the same way as anybody else to be admitted. After being admitted, you can become eligible for a scholarship.

The school might be able to give you financial aid. Make sure to check thoroughly your school's website to see which kind of financial aid it offers.

You may also receive financial aid from another organization that can pay your universities expenses. For example, the MasterCard Foundation gives scholarships to many undergraduate and graduate students in different countries around the world.
You can request a student loan that you will have to pay after graduation. If you have the money, you can always pay for yourself.


College readiness programs

If you can prepare yourself for college applications on your own, that's great! However, that can be hard for many people, especially if your English is not good. There are organizations that help high school students or high school graduates to prepare for the standardized tests and guide students through the university application process.

These programs prepare students to apply to universities when they are still in high school:
  • Bridge2Rwanda : Two years before finishing high school, students spend a summer in Rwanda learning English and Leadership. The program follows up with the students when they go back to school so that they can be ready to apply to universities as soon as they graduate from high school
  • Meto
  • United World College
  • African Leadership Academy

If you have already graduated from high school, you can apply to

  • Ashinaga: Gap-year program with a French program in Sngal and an English program in Uganda.
  • Tujenge: English gap-year program similar to Ashinaga and Bridge2Rwanda. It is based in Bujumbura.

To apply to graduate school, you will need:
  • Your university transcript
  • Two or three recommendation letters
  • Statement of purpose
  • Personal statement
  • Other essays required by the program that you are applying to
Here are some other facts that you can keep in mind as you prepare your application:
  • Researching while you are still in undergraduate school will look great on your graduate application
  • Work hard to have the best grades you can have in undergraduate studies. It will show that you are skilled and can handle the workload of graduate school
  • Be active in the real world. You can show that through volunteering, work, internship, ...
  • Unlike undergraduate school, you should know what you want to study in graduate school because, in your application, you need to say which program you are applying to and why. If you are applying to a PhD program, you should know different research projects going at the universities that you are applying to, and if possible, get in touch with professors working on those projects.
  • Some schools might require another exam called GRE. For more information about GRE, click here.

Financial aid

There are not as many need-based scholarships for graduate school as for undergraduate. However, each school has some kind of financial aid that you might be eligible for. If you apply for a PhD, it is likely to be fully funded because the project that you work on will have a dedicated budget that will cover your living expenses.
For a master's degree, you can be a teaching or research assistant, and the school can cover some, if not most of your expenses.

There are some organizations that can fund your graduate education:
  • Erasmus offered by the European Union has fully funded master's programs in Europe
  • Chevening also offers master's scholarships in the United Kingdom
  • MasterCard Foundation offers scholarships in different countries around the world
  • ARES (Acadmie de Recherche et d'Enseignement Suprieur) : As a partner of Belgian Development Cooperation, ARES provides, for nationals of his 29 partners countries, scholarships for bachelors and masters degree programmes, and continuing education courses in Belgium, which feature training that specifically addresses issues related to development.
